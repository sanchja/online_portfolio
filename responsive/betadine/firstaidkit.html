First Aid kit



Medical Book

A basic first aid book is an important source of information, treatment procedures, and instruction to keep handy.


Betadine Skin Cleanser
(Povidone-iodine, 7.5%)

You don’t want to tend a wound with dirty hands. Degerm before (and after) tending a wound with Betadine® Skin Cleanser.
(Povidone-iodine, 7.5%)


Rubber Gloves

Slip on a pair of sanitized gloves to keep you and the person you’re helping safe and clean.


Pain Reliever

Aspirin, acetaminophen, and ibuprofen are popular over-the-counter pain relievers used to relieve pain and bring down fever. (Aspirin should not be given to anyone under age 18 because of the danger of Reye's syndrome.)


Gauze

Gauze pads can be used to apply pressure to cuts and scrapes until the bleeding subsides. With tape, gauze can also be used as a bandage to cover and protect wounds (remember to let dry first).


Tape

Adhesive tape can be used to attach small and large bandages (remember to let dry first). It can also help create pressure over a wound to free hands for other first aid.


Bandage Scissors

Dedicate a pair of angled-blade bandage scissors (also known as trauma shears) to neatly cut adhesive tape without cutting your patient.


Cold Pack

Cold packs can relieve pain, swelling, and inflammation for sprains, strains, and other everyday injuries.


First Aid Bag

Keep all your first aid essentials in one place with a dedicated bag—so you can find what you need when you need it.


Hydrocortisone Cream

In the garden, on the trail, or around the world—rashes, bites, and burns can happen anywhere. A topical steroid can reduce inflammation.
