Recommendations for Surgical Hand Scrubbing4,11


To assure maximum antimicrobial activity, Purdue Products L.P. recommends the use of Betadine® products at full strength only. The patient’s skin should be scrubbed with Betadine® Surgical Scrub first, followed by the application of Betadine® Solution or Betadine® Spray. Due to its detergent content, the Betadine® Surgical Scrub needs to be rinsed off before the Betadine® Solution or Betadine® Spray is applied.


Recommendation for preoperative preparation of a patient is:


  1. Wet hands with water and spread about 5cc
    (1 teaspoonful) of Betadine® Surgical Scrub over both hands and forearms.
  2. Without adding more water, scrub thoroughly for
    2.5 to 3 minutes.
  3. Use a brush if desired. Clean thoroughly under fingernails.
  4. Add a little water to develop copious suds. Rinse under running water.
  5. Repeat steps 1-4 for another 2.5 to 3 minutes

Use of Betadine® products in Surgical Patients4,11

Preparation of the patient’s skin before surgery with an effective antiseptic is an important tool for the reduction of postoperative surgical site infections. The Association of Perioperative Registered Nurses (AORN) “Recommended Practices for Skin Preparation of Patients” states that the goal of preoperative skin preparation is to remove soil and transient microorganisms from the skin, reduce the resident microbial count to subpathogenic levels in a short period of time and with the least amount of tissue irritation, and inhibit rapid rebound growth of microorganisms.


Povidone-iodine is a commonly used agent for preoperative preparation of the skin.


Recommendations for Preoperative Skin Preparation of Patients4,11


Before skin preparation of a patient is initiated, the skin should be free of soil and debris. Hair is best left at the surgical site. Preoperative shaving is associated with increased risk of SSIs. If it is necessary to remove hair, it should be done in a manner that preserves skin integrity (e.g., clipping).


The patient’s skin is prepared by applying the antiseptic in concentric circles, progressing away from the intended incision site to the periphery. The prepared area should be large enough to allow for additional incisions or drain sites, if necessary.


To assure maximum antimicrobial activity, Purdue Products L.P. recommends the use of Betadine® products at full strength only. The patient’s skin should be scrubbed with Betadine® Surgical Scrub first, followed by the application of Betadine® Solution or Betadine® Spray. Due to its detergent content, the Betadine® Surgical Scrub needs to be rinsed off before the Betadine® Solution or Betadine® Spray is applied.


Recommendation for preoperative preparation of a patient is:


  1. Wet area with water
  2. Apply Betadine® Surgical Scrub (1 ml is sufficient to cover an area 20-30 square inches)
  3. Develop lather and scrub thoroughly for 5 minutes
  4. Rinse off with sterile gauze saturated with water
  5. Apply Betadine® Solution (concentric circles from inside out) or spray with Betadine® Spray
  6. Allow area to dry

It is recommended that Betadine® Solution be allowed to air dry following application. This ensures that povidone-iodine is in contact with the skin long enough to provide full antimicrobial effect.


Warning: Avoid pooling of Betadine® Solution beneath the patient or in intertriginous folds or trapping of solution under a dressing. Prolonged exposure to wet solution may cause irritation, burns, or in rare instances, severe skin reactions.

For external use only.

Do not use in the eyes, over large areas of the body, or if you are sensitive to iodine or other product ingredients. Ask a doctor before use in case of deep or puncture wounds, animal bites, or serious burns. Stop use and ask a doctor if the condition persists or gets worse or if you need to use this product for more than 1 week. Keep out of reach of children. If swallowed, get medical help or contact a Poison Control Center right away.
